Emotional Intelligence

11 September 2023

  1. What is Emotional Intelligence?

    Emotional Intelligence (EQ) is the ability to handle emotions, of both yourself and others. It's like being in tune with your own feelings, keeping them in check when necessary, staying motivated, getting what's going on in other people's heads, and handling social stuff well.

  2. How is EQ different to IQ?

    IQ is book smarts, stuff like math, logic, and puzzles. EQ, on the other hand, is street smarts, all about your emotional and social skills - how well you understand and handle your own feelings and those of others. While IQ tends to remain relatively stable as you get older, EQ can be developed and refined, making it a vital contributor to overall success, as it empowers individuals to navigate emotions, collaborate effectively, and lead fulfilling lives.

  3. Why is EQ important?

    EQ is important for career growth and learning because it enhances your interpersonal skills, emotional resilience, adaptability, and overall effectiveness in the workplace. It empowers you to navigate complex social and emotional dynamics, which are inherent in professional settings, and positions you for success and personal development.
