Foundations Reflection

27 September 2023

  • What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

    1. I find genuine satisfaction in working with and assisting others - helping to solve a problem, offering guidance, or providing support, that sense of satisfaction encourages me to keep helping and being a valuable resource for those around me.
    2. Taking pride in my work motivates me to ensure that it meets high standards and reflects the dedication and effort I put into it. This mindset pushes me to consistently deliver my best work.
    3. I have a strong sense of attention to detail which ensures that the final output is polished and accurate, ensuring I meet the specific requirements and exceed expectations where possible.

  • What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

    Values guide decisions and define professional identity, empathy enables better team collaboration and user focussed solutions, and self-awareness enables effective learning and personal growth.

  • What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

    I was surprised by the strong focus on personal growth and self-awareness throughout the Foundations Core Skills program. It was eye-opening to learn that tech employers prioritize exceptional human qualities and cultural fit, shifting my perspective on what makes a valuable candidate. Additionally, researching neuroplasticity, growth mindsets, and honing effective communication skills like active listening highlighted the vital role of mindset and interpersonal abilities in the tech industry. The holistic approach to learning and problem-solving, surpassed my expectations, emphasizing the need to be a well-rounded professional beyond just technical competence.

  • What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

    Introspection was challenging for me at first; delving into my emotions and interpreting them into something I could write down is something I've always struggled with, but I feel like I have got much better and less self conscious with sharing those feelings. Establishing consistent habits like daily meditation and self-reflection presented their own set of difficulties; initially, finding the time and committing to these practices was a struggle, but with perseverance and determination, I successfully managed to make them a part of my routine, and I've noticed the benefits.

  • Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

    The core learning promotes holistic skill development by focussing on problem-solving, effective communication, and teamwork, preparing students not just to be coders but also to be adaptable professionals which is essential in such a fast changing industry.

  • Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead? Justify your answer.

    Dedicating time to the core/soft skills has been really useful and is essential because they are the building blocks of effective collaboration and communication. Skills like adaptability, empathy, and teamwork create a positive work environment and enhance employability, job satisfaction, and leadership potential, which will all be crucial in my future career progression.
