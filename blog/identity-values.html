My Identity, Values, and Strengths

06 September 2023

  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

    Given the recent AI hype, I've found myself faced with an ethical dilemma: should I use ChatGPT to make my school life easier? Reflecting on it, my values of honesty and integrity came into play. I realized that using AI as a shortcut in my studies would undermine the whole point of learning. Learning isn't just about gaining knowledge; it's about the journey of growing intellectually. Skipping the critical thinking part with AI wouldn't be fair to myself, and it wouldn't align with my values. Plus, I've always believed in taking pride in my work. There's something immensely satisfying about knowing that my academic achievements are a result of my own effort and understanding. Relying on AI would cheapen that sense of accomplishment. So, I've made the ethical decision to stick to the old-fashioned way of studying, even though AI is tempting. It allows me to truly engage with my studies, develop my critical thinking skills, and, most importantly, maintain my integrity.

  2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

    Growing up in the UK, I was surrounded by a supportive community that instilled in me the values of honesty, integrity, and hard work. During my travels since leaving the UK, living in New Zealand for the past 10 years, I've noticed some similarities and differences. In both places, there's a strong emphasis on values like honesty and integrity. However, New Zealanders seem to prioritize health, wellbeing, and a deep love for the natural environment even more. So, my background in the UK laid the foundation for my values, while New Zealand has deepened my appreciation for health, nature, and a well-rounded life.

  3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

    My strengths include being a natural problem solver and critical thinker. I'm also quite persistent, which drives me to overcome challenges and learn new things effectively. Additionally, I enjoy assisting others who may be facing similar obstacles. These qualities will support me in my learning journey by enabling me to tackle challenges head-on and contribute positively to the learning community.

  4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

    Over the last 12 months I've been working on addressing my tendency to overthink, which can hinder decision-making and delay my academic and career progress. I'm also working on boosting my self-confidence, as it impacts active participation, effective communication, and is essential when it comes to networking and career development.

  5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

    In my previous role as a business analyst, I often faced resistance from stakeholders who feared that my work would replace their processes with computer systems. To address this, I used empathy and reassurance, emphasizing process enhancement rather than replacement and encouraging their input. While these strategies generally eased tensions and promoted collaboration, I now realize that involving stakeholders from the project's start would have been even more effective in reducing initial resistance and fostering collaboration sooner.
